How The Audit Works

The Audit reads your OPay, Moniepoint, or GTBank bank statement PDF directly in your browser and turns months of transactions into a single financial health score. Nothing is uploaded to a server — your statement is parsed locally on your device, so your data never leaves your phone or computer.

  1. Upload — Choose a PDF bank statement. Password-protected statements are supported.
  2. Parse — The Audit identifies every transaction, categorizes it (transfers, airtime, food, bills, savings, and more), and builds a month-by-month picture of your cash flow.
  3. Score — A financial health score is calculated from your savings rate, spending consistency, and category balance.
  4. Plan — A personalized budget plan is generated based on what you actually spend, not generic percentages.

What Your Score Measures

Your financial score isn't just about how much you save. It weighs several factors pulled straight from your statement:

  • Savings rate — the share of inflow you keep rather than spend.
  • Spending consistency — how stable your monthly spending is versus erratic spikes.
  • Category balance — whether spending is concentrated in one area (e.g. transfers or betting) or spread sensibly.
  • Income reliability — how regular your inflows are month to month.

Understanding Your Spending Categories

The Audit doesn't just total your spending — it breaks it down into categories so you can see where your money actually goes, not just how much left your account.

  • Savings / Investment — money moved into savings plans, auto-save features, or investment vehicles like OWealth. This is the category most tied to long-term financial health.
  • Transfers Out — funds sent to other people or accounts. High transfer volume isn't necessarily bad, but erratic spikes often signal impulsive spending.
  • Transfers In — money received, whether from salary, side income, or family. Consistency here matters more than the total amount.
  • Airtime & Data — recurring spend on mobile airtime and data bundles, often a small but steady drain worth tracking.
  • Fees & Charges — bank levies, USSD charges, SMS alerts, and VAT. Individually small, but they add up over months.
  • Other — transactions that don't cleanly fit elsewhere, flagged so nothing gets buried.

Each category gets its own bar, total, and transaction list, so you can drill down into exactly which transactions are driving your score.

Why Bank Statement Analysis Matters

Most budgeting advice assumes a fixed monthly salary and predictable expenses. That's rarely how money actually moves for most Nigerians — income often comes from a mix of salary, side hustles, and transfers from family or clients, while spending is split across mobile money platforms like OPay and Moniepoint, bank apps, and cash withdrawals.

A single bank statement can't capture that whole picture, but it can reveal patterns that matter more than the raw numbers: how consistent your income is, how much of it actually gets saved, and whether spending spikes are occasional or a habit. The Audit reads through those patterns automatically, so you don't have to scroll through pages of transactions to find them yourself.

A high income with no savings discipline can score lower than a modest income with consistent saving habits — because financial health isn't just about how much you earn, it's about how predictably you manage what comes in.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my bank statement data safe?

Yes. Parsing happens entirely in your browser using JavaScript — your PDF is never sent to any server, including ours.

Which banks are supported?

OPay, Moniepoint, and GTBank statements are currently supported, with more Nigerian banks being added over time.

Why does my score look low even though I save money?

The score blends multiple factors. A high savings rate can still be pulled down by erratic spending patterns or unreliable income, since long-term financial health depends on consistency as much as the amount saved.

Can I use this if my statement is password-protected?

Yes, you'll be prompted to enter the PDF password before parsing begins.
